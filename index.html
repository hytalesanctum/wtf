Clothes Modelling

41 languages

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Clothes modelling (also called fashion modelling) is a profession where individuals model clothing and fashion designs created by fashion designers and fashion houses.

Fashion models showcase the latest clothing designs on the runway

Definition[edit]

Clothes modelling is the profession of displaying clothing and fashion accessories to the public. Models work for fashion designers, photographers, and marketing agencies to promote and advertise fashion collections. They appear in various media including runway shows, print advertising, television commercials, and digital platforms.

History[edit]

The fashion modelling industry emerged in the late 19th century with the rise of haute couture. Early fashion models were primarily used for in-store displays and print advertising. The modern modelling industry took shape during the 20th century with the establishment of major modelling agencies.

The supermodel era began in the 1960s with iconic figures such as Twiggy and Jean Shrimpton. These models became cultural icons and influenced fashion trends significantly. Today, fashion modelling is a multi-billion dollar global industry.

Career Requirements[edit]

Professional models typically meet specific physical requirements, though standards vary by market and agency:

  • Specific height and body measurements
  • Clear complexion and good skin health
  • Professional demeanor and reliability
  • Portfolio of professional photographs
  • Ability to follow direction from photographers and stylists

Challenges[edit]

The modelling industry faces numerous criticisms regarding unrealistic beauty standards, mental health issues among models, and exploitative practices. There are ongoing discussions about diversity and inclusion in the fashion industry.
